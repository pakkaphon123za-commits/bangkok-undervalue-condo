# Bangkok Condo Market Brief

An analysis of 9,599 condominium transactions located within 500 meters of Bangkok’s mass transit stations reveals that 6.91% of properties are trading below their model‑based value, indicating selective mispricing across the network. The strongest signal comes from the **BTS Sukhumvit Line**, which alone accounts for 387 undervalued units (8.85% of its transactions) and exhibits the steepest price decay with distance from stations (−26.2% per km). This implies that premiums for proximity to Sukhumvit stations are highly concentrated, and investors can capture significant discounts within a short walk of the station exit.

The **MRT Pink Line** posts the highest line‑level undervaluation rate at 12.9%, while the **SRT Dark Red Line** (10.26%) and **Airport Rail Link** (7.88%) also exceed the global average. At the station level, discounts are most extreme at **Phetkasem 48** (MRT Blue Line, 54.84% undervalued, 66.24% median discount) and **Bearing** (BTS Sukhumvit Line, 37.04% undervalued, 57.52% median discount), though both have limited transaction counts. The median residual across the market is near zero (0.013085 log‑points), but the dispersion (MAD 0.297672) confirms that pricing accuracy varies substantially, creating opportunities for value‑focused buyers.

Investors targeting undervalued assets should concentrate on lines where undervaluation rates exceed the global 6.91% benchmark and price decay is steep—particularly the BTS Sukhumvit Line, where a cluster of stations from On Nut to Bearing offers a defined corridor of mispricing. Upcoming expansions of the MRT Orange, MRT Purple South, and SRT Dark Red south extensions will extend both the network and the potential for revaluation in adjacent submarkets, though transaction data for those corridors is not yet captured in this analysis.

## Per‑line analysis

- **Airport Rail Link:** 7.88% of properties are undervalued, with a price decay of −17.53% per km—moderate localization of station premiums.  
- **BTS Gold Line:** Only 1.79% undervalued, the lowest line‑wide share, paired with very steep decay (−23.38%), indicating extreme sensitivity to station distance.  
- **BTS Silom Line:** 3.36% undervalued and the shallowest decay among BTS lines (−14.48%), suggesting more uniform pricing along the route.  
- **BTS Sukhumvit Line:** 8.85% undervalued in absolute terms (387 units), and the steepest overall decay (−26.2% per km), making it the primary hunting ground for bargains.  
- **MRT Blue Line:** 5.27% undervalued overall, with decay of −19.45%, but containing two of the most discounted station zones (Phetkasem 48 and Huai Khwang).  
- **MRT Pink Line:** 12.9% undervalued (the highest line‑wide rate) with moderate decay (−11.73%), yet derived from only 124 observations.  
- **MRT Purple Line:** Zero of 73 transactions are classified as undervalued, implying no properties trade below the global threshold on this line.  
- **MRT Yellow Line:** 6.98% undervalued, with decay of −12.44%—very close to the global average undervaluation rate.  
- **SRT Dark Red Line:** 10.26% undervalued, decay −15.2%, a high rate but a very small sample (39 transactions).  
- **SRT Light Red Line:** 4.55% undervalued (benchmarked to global statistics) and the gentlest decay (−10.1% per km).

## Undervalued station zones

The ten most undervalued stations are heavily concentrated on the **BTS Sukhumvit Line**, which supplies seven of them. **Bang Na** leads the line in both undervaluation share (24.3%) and median discount (60.98%), followed by **Bearing** (37.04% and 57.52%) and **Punnawithi** (20.47% and 49.53%). Closer to the city center, **Nana** (15.79%) and **On Nut** (13.92%) also trade well below model predictions. The presence of **Udom Suk**, **Phra Khanong**, and **Huai Khwang** (MRT Blue Line) reinforces a pattern where intermediate stations along the Sukhumvit corridor and the western edge of the MRT Blue Line offer the deepest discounts. **Phetkasem 48** on the MRT Blue Line records the highest station‑level undervaluation rate (54.84%) and the largest median discount (66.24%), but the thin sample (31 transactions) calls for caution. **Ramkhamhaeng** (Airport Rail Link, 8.41% undervalued, 53.32% median discount) provides an additional value play on a line with fewer transaction counts.

## Upcoming line impact

The pending completion of the **MRT Orange Line**, the **MRT Purple South extension**, and the **SRT Dark Red south extension** will significantly alter connectivity patterns. Historically, new lines have narrowed mispricing gaps as improved access raises demand in previously overlooked areas. Investors should watch stations near these future routes for signs of revaluation, particularly where current undervaluation is high and the network gap is set to close. No transaction data for these specific corridors is available in this analysis.

**Methodology:** Properties are classified as undervalued if their log‑price residual from a hedonic model (controlling for location, distance to station, floor area, and other unit characteristics) falls below the global median residual of 0.013085 (MAD 0.297672).
